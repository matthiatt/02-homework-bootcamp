About Me


Background Information:


I was born and raised in Illinois. For the most part I lived in Chicago until my parents got divorced. Once they got divorced, I then moved to the suburbs; specifically by Roselle, Illinois. My mom was a project manager for a company called - Tellabs. They were a communcations company, but they are no longer around today. My father was a labour, he usually went from job to job, whatever was paying the highest for work, he never specialized in a type of skill.


Eventually I went to a high school at Lake Park, which was in Roselle and Medinah. I didn't move again, the school is so massive that it had two campuses. The one in Roselle was considered to be west campus, while east campus was in Medinah. While there, I played football for the school. I played on the defensive line until I lost 100 pounds, then I played middle line-backer. I also played baseball, but I didn't play for the school, I played for a travel team after football season.


After my time at Lake Park High School, I decided that I wanted to go to college to further my education. I got accepted to a vast amount of colleges, but unfortunatly I could only pick in-state colleges at the time(that's all my mother could offord. - I am very gratful though I got an oppertunity). Out of all the colleges I applied to, I decided to become a Red Bird at Illinois State University. It would be there where I would find myself, my long-time friends, and memories I still hold till this day.


At Illinois State I tried a lot of majors to see what I would really enjoy. At first it was Kinesiology(the study of anatomy), but that didn't work for me. I found out that I prefer to have that as a hobby, rather than a career. There were a lot of other majors I tried, but after a year and a half I found a subject that I enjoyed, English Education. I enjoyed English becuase it taught people how to critically think, use context to support claims, attention to detail, and if you can effectivly read, then you can practically teach yourself anything you want. I ended up not wanting to teach though after I completed my clinical hours.


In 2017 I graduated with my degree in English Education, the first in my family to graduate from college so my mom was super proud of me. I didn't want to stop my education there though. Not only becuase I didn't want to be a teacher, but becuase I had a love for learning. To me, knowledge is more valuable than anything else in this world. I like to tell people that I value knowledge over money. Money comes and money goes, but once someone has the knowledge to do something, then no one can take that away from you; it's simply priceless.


So then my next chapter in my life started, I was going to get my 2ed degree. I already knew what I wanted to study next - Political Science. A lot of people give me weird looks when I tell them this, but there are a lot of reasons why I decided to make this my second major.


  1. #1: You learn about psychology - how do people react and why?
  2. #2: You learn about sociology - demographics, culture, and barriers.
  3. #3: You learn about economics - trade and policy.
  4. #4: You learn about philosophy - This one is my favorite. This teaches you on questioning the basic fundamentals of your mental state. Makes you think outside the box on how to solve problems and learn how to problem solve in a unique manner.
